5.4 Probabilistic analysis and further uses of indicator random variables


Exercises


5.4-1
How many people must there be in a room before the probability that someone has the same birthday as you do is at least 1/2? How many people must there be before the probability that at least two people have a birthday on July 4 is greater than 1/2?


  1. 나와 다른 생일을 가진 사람이 있을 확률
    $$\frac{n-1}{n}$$

    나와 다른 생일을 가진 사람이 k명 있을 확률 (1은 맨 처음에 각기 다른 날짜로 생일을 고를 확률이다.)
    $$1\cdot\left(\frac{n-1}{n}\right)^{k}$$

    식
    $$ 1 - \left (\frac{n-1}{n}\right )^{k} \geq \frac{1}{2}, \quad \left (\frac{n-1}{n}\right )^{k}  \geq \frac{1}{2}, \\ \left (\frac{364}{365}\right )^{k} \geq \frac{1}{2}, \quad k\lg \left(\frac{364}{365}\right) \geq \lg \left(\frac{1}{2}\right), \quad k \geq \frac{\lg \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)}{\lg \left(\frac{364}{365}\right)} \approx 253 $$
    따라서, 같은 생일을 가진 사람이 적어도 1/2 있어야 하려면, 최소 253명이 방 안에 있어야 한다.



  2. $$ \begin{align} \text{Pr{2 born on July 4}} &= 1 - \Pr\{\text{1 born on Jul 4}\} - Pr\{\text{0 born on Jul4}\} \\ &= 1-\binom{k}{1}\left ( \frac{n-1}{n} \right )^{k-1} - \left ( \frac{n-1}{n} \right )^{k}  \end{align} $$

    $$ 1-\left ( \frac{364}{365} \right )^{k-1}\left ( \frac{364+k}{365} \right ) > \frac{1}{2}, \quad \left ( \frac{364}{365} \right )^{k-1}\left ( \frac{364+k}{365} \right ) < \frac{1}{2} $$

    계산기로 계산해보면 k ≒ 613 이다.

5.4-3 ★
For the analysis of the birthday paradox, is it important that the birthdays be mutually independent, or is pairwise independence sufficient? Justify your answer.

  • $$\text{Pr}\left \{ b_{i}=b_{j} \right\} = \sum_{r=1}^{n}\text{Pr}\left \{ b_{i}=r \ \ \text{and}\ \ b_{j}=r \right\}$$
    Obviously, it involves only two variables $b_{i}$ and $b_{j}$. Thus, pairwise independence is sufficient.





5.4-4 ★
How many people should be invited to a party in order to make it likely that there are three people with the same birthday?


\[\binom{k}{3}\cdot\frac{1}{n^{2}} = \frac{k(k-1)(k-2)}{3!}\cdot\frac{1}{n^{2}}=\frac{k(k-1)(k-2)}{6n^{2}}\]

\[\frac{k(k-1)(k-2)}{6\cdot365^{2}} \geq 1, \quad k\approx 94\]






5.4-5 ★
What is the probability that a k-string over a set of size n forms a k-permutation? How does this question relate to the birthday paradox?


\[\text{Pr}\{\text{k-perm in n} \} = 1\cdot\frac{n-1}{n}\cdot\frac{n-2}{n}\cdots\frac{n-k+1}{n}=\frac{_{n}\text{P}_{r}}{n^{k}}\]
이것은 k명의 사람이 각기 다른 생일을 가지는 확률이다.






5.4-6 ★
Suppose that n balls are tossed into n bins, where each toss is independent and the ball is equally likely to end up in any bin. What is the expected number of empty bins? What is the expected number of bins with exactly one ball?

$$X_{i} = I\{ \text{bin i is empty}\} \\ E[X_{i}] = Pr\{X_{i}\} = \left ( \frac{n-1}{n} \right )^{n}\approx \frac{1}{e} \\ E[X]=\sum_{i=1}^{n}E[X_{i}]\approx \frac{n}{e}$$

$$Y_{i} = I\{ \text{bin i contains exactly one ball}\} \\ E[Y_{i}] = Pr\{Y_{i}\} = \binom{n}{1}\frac{1}{n}\left ( \frac{n-1}{n} \right )^{n-1}=\left ( \frac{n-1}{n} \right )^{n-1} \approx \frac{1}{e}\\ E[Y]=\sum_{i=1}^{n}E[Y_{i}]\approx \frac{n}{e}$$
